The applicant's journey — “your form has been submitted”… then what?

A citizen applies to a government program (e.g. the Ministry of Youth science summer camp) through the GovTech Forms app. Their application moves into the case management system for review — but from where they stand, almost all of that is invisible. The intended experience: submit a form, get told you can track it, check a page, and be notified when something changes. This map shows how far today's experience is from that.

Pain pointsWhere the journey breaks

No expectations set

Nothing tells them when — or how — they'll hear back. Rolling vs batch decisions make one honest answer per program necessary.

Mismatched reference numbers

The Forms app ID isn't the case management ID — a support conversation waiting to happen.

The tracking page has no entryway

The page exists; the link is never given out. The Forms app doesn't yet know the application went on to case management, so its email can't explain where to look.

Text only

If the team needs a document, this loop can't carry it — attachments only work on the original form.

Silent decisions, dead-end rejections

No email on approval or rejection. Rejection copy offers no reason, no contact and no next step.

No closure

“Closed” is the last word. Nothing summarises the outcome or points to what they could do next time.

OpportunitiesWhat would fix it

Set expectations at submit

Tell them up front how this program decides: “you'll hear back in X days” or “this form typically responds in X”.

Open question

Share the tracking link

The URL is constructible today. Put it in the confirmation email — while being wary that a page that mostly says “nothing has changed” can generate demand instead of absorbing it.

Open question

Allow documents in responses

Described as not much more work — it just hasn't been requested yet.

Email on every status change

The CMS can already send email; wiring it to status changes is “very straightforward”. Pair it with rejection copy that gives a reason and a contact route.

Close with dignity

A final message that states the outcome, thanks them, and says what (if anything) they can do next.

The intended experience

  • Fill in a form.
  • Get told you can track your application somewhere.
  • Go to a page and see the status.
  • Get notified when more information is needed or the status changes.

That shape is stable across services. Today, only the middle of it exists — and the applicant can't reach it.

The core problem

  • After the submission email, the service never contacts the applicant again — except to ask them for something.
  • Every state change (received, approved, rejected, archived) happens on a page they were never given the link to.
  • Both push (email on change) and pull (a self-serve status page) have utility — the transcript's conclusion is to do both.

Questions to answer next

  • Who owns the post-submission relationship — the Forms app or the case management system?
  • What does honest expectation-setting look like for batch-decided programs (e.g. summer camp allocating everyone at once)?
  • What should rejection say, and what route does an unsuccessful applicant get?
