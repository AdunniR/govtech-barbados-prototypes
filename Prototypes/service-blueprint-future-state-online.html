Temporary restaurant licence & Environmental Health Officer request — the digitised process

Ministry of Health — Environmental Health Department × GovTech Barbados. The launch-scope process: applicants apply once, online, on alpha.gov.bb; the application is routed by the event's catchment and emailed to the correct polyclinic's role-based EHD mailbox. Review, inspection and approval stay with polyclinic staff — unchanged — while travel, collection trips and the Google Forms workaround disappear. Case management comes later.

New

Removed from the journey

  • Trip 1 — applying in person at the event-catchment polyclinic, wherever on the island that is.
  • Trip 2 — collecting the licence in person after a phone call.
  • The Google Forms workaround: the 31-email monthly cap, the personal-account access, the daily thumb-drive backups.
  • Front-desk transcription and redirection of applicants who came to the wrong polyclinic.
  • The “why do you need an officer” question — attendance is mandatory when food is served, so the form states it instead of asking.
  • Itemised officer rates shown to organizers, and the negotiation that followed.
  • Handwritten signatures — replaced by the checkbox declaration.

New in the journey

  • Content pages listing everything needed before the form starts — definitions, stall requirements, sample stall plan, costs.
  • Automatic catchment routing to the correct polyclinic's role-based EHD mailbox.
  • Branching between the two forms so organizers are never left without the officer request.
  • Reference numbers, confirmation emails and a scan-to-verify digital licence.
  • Site plan collected up front (organizers only) so layout problems are fixed before event day.
  • QR codes and multi-channel outreach ahead of the Crop Over peak.
  • Structured data across all seven polyclinics from day one.

Deliberately unchanged

  • The two-week application window — it exists so officers can inspect food preparation before the event.
  • Clerk → senior → principal review, including the fast track for already-licensed food businesses and cross-catchment inspection requests.
  • The site visit and early layout conversation with the organizer.
  • Officer discretion on event day, including the power to close a stall.
  • Who decides: content approvals sit with the Chief; officer allocation sits with the principal and senior.
  • Email delivery to polyclinics at launch — the case management system (status tracking, cross-polyclinic views) is a later phase, not this one.
